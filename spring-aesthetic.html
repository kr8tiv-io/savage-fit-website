6-Week Timeline

Weeks 1-2: Foundation & Activation

Build the foundation with glute activation drills, form mastery, and establishing mind-muscle connection. Volume: moderate. Focus: technique and muscle engagement.

Weeks 3-4: Progressive Overload

Increase training intensity and volume. Progressive overload kicks in. Heavier weights, more sets, strategic fatigue. This is where growth accelerates.

Weeks 5-6: Peak Volume & Deload

Week 5 pushes peak training volume — maximum stimulus for growth. Week 6 includes a strategic deload to allow recovery and supercompensation before the challenge ends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I do this at home, or do I need a gym?

This program is designed for gym training with access to barbells, dumbbells, cables, and machines. While some exercises can be modified for home training, you'll get the best results with full gym access.

I'm a beginner. Can I still join?

If you're brand new to lifting, we recommend starting with Savage Society or the Savage Lifestyle challenge to build a foundation first.

This challenge assumes you have basic lifting experience and understand compound movements like squats, deadlifts, and hip thrusts.

What happens after the 6 weeks?

You'll have built a solid glute foundation and (if you're in the Full tier) receive 1 month of Savage Society to continue your training.

Many women then transition to ongoing Savage Society membership, join the next seasonal challenge, or apply for 1-on-1 coaching.

Will I lose fat or just build muscle?

This is a muscle-building program. The goal is glute hypertrophy — adding size and shape.

Whether you lose fat depends on your nutrition. The program includes macro guidelines for muscle gain (slight surplus) or recomposition (maintenance calories). If your goal is fat loss, check out the Savage Shred challenge instead.
